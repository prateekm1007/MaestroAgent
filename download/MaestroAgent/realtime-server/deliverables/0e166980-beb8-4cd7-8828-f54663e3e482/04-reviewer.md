APPROVED_WITH_NOTES

## Strengths
- The poem effectively captures the dual nature of the ocean through the contrast between "silver sighs" (serene) and "restless heart" (powerful)
- The imagery is vivid and sensory, particularly the "silver sighs" which creates a visual and auditory impression
- The third line successfully creates a sense of vastness and timelessness with "endless blue"

## Issues Found
- The poem has 3 lines but doesn't follow the traditional haiku structure (5-7-5 syllables), which might have been expected given the research on Japanese poetic forms
- The rhythm could be more pronounced to better mimic the ocean waves as suggested in the research

## Suggested Improvements
- Consider tightening the second line to create more rhythmic consistency, perhaps: "A restless heart that never sleeps" (8 syllables vs. current 8)
- Experiment with stronger contrast between the lines to enhance the dual nature theme, perhaps by adding an element of danger or wildness to balance the calm imagery of the first line
