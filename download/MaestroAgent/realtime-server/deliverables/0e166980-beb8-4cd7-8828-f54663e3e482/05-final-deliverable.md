## Summary
Ocean poetry often captures the duality of the sea—both serene and powerful, life-giving and destructive. This research explores common themes, imagery, and structures used in concise ocean poems to inform a 3-line composition.

## Key Findings
- **Rhythm and Flow**: Successful ocean poems often use rhythmic patterns that mimic waves, with alternating stressed and unstressed syllables creating a natural cadence
- **Dual Nature Imagery**: Effective poems contrast opposing ocean elements—vastness vs. intimacy, power vs. calm, surface vs. depth—to create tension and depth
- **Sensory Language**: The most impactful ocean poems engage multiple senses—sight (blue expanse), sound (crashing waves), and touch (salt spray)—to immerse the reader
