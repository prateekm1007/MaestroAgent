## Summary
Research notes on benefits of working from home, focusing on productivity, cost savings, and well-being.

## Key Findings
- **Productivity Boost**: Multiple studies show remote workers often report higher productivity due to fewer office distractions and personalized work environments. Stanford research found a 13% productivity increase among remote workers.
- **Cost Savings**: Employees save significantly on commuting, work attire, and daily meals, with average annual savings of $4,000-$6,000. Companies also benefit from reduced overhead costs for office space.
- **Improved Well-being**: Flexible schedules and eliminated commutes reduce stress and improve work-life balance. Gallup reports remote workers experience better emotional health and lower burnout rates.
- **Environmental Impact**: Remote work reduces carbon emissions through decreased commuting and office energy consumption, contributing to sustainability goals.
- **Talent Access**: Companies can hire from global talent pools without geographic limitations, leading to more diverse and skilled teams.

- HR and management publications on talent acquisition and retention in remote settings
